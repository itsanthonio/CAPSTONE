Platform Capabilities

AI Detection Pipeline

FPN-ResNet50 processes 6-band HLS imagery into per-pixel mining probability maps. Detections are vectorised, area-filtered, and cross-checked against legal concession boundaries.

Field Verification

Alerts are dispatched to field inspectors who verify detections on the ground. Evidence photos, visit dates, and outcomes feed back into live model accuracy tracking.

Automated Scanning

A Ghana-wide scan grid runs automatically 6am–6pm daily, prioritising known hotspot regions and scheduling work via a Celery task queue backed by Google Earth Engine.

Understanding the Crisis

What is Galamsey?

The Human Cost

Galamsey — from "gather them and sell" — is illegal artisanal gold mining operating outside licensed concessions. Unregulated operations discharge mercury and cyanide directly into rivers, threatening the drinking water of millions of Ghanaians living downstream and poisoning fishing communities that depend on the water.

Ecosystems Under Threat

The Pra, Offin, and Ankobra river basins — once clean — have suffered severe water-quality degradation. The Atewa Forest Reserve in Ghana's Eastern Region, a globally significant biodiversity hotspot and the source of three major rivers, faces ongoing encroachment from illegal miners pushing ever deeper into the forest.

SankofaWatch was built at Ashesi University, Berekuso, Ghana — named for the Sankofa bird that flies forward while looking back, a reminder that protecting Ghana's future means learning from and restoring its past.
