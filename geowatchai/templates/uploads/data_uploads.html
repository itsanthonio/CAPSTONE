Legal Concessions — GeoJSON Upload

Boundaries of licensed mining concessions from the Minerals Commission. Uploading replaces all existing concessions and automatically reclassifies every detection as legal or illegal.

Drop GeoJSON file here or click to browse

Accepted: .geojson, .json  ·  Polygon or MultiPolygon features

What happens on upload

  1. 1 All existing concession boundaries are replaced with the new file
  2. 2 Every detection is checked against the new boundaries using a spatial intersection
  3. 3 Detections inside a concession → Legal. Outside → Illegal
  4. 4 The live map updates immediately to reflect the new classification

Expected GeoJSON properties: license_number, name, holder_name, license_type. Missing fields use sensible defaults.

River & Water Body Boundaries — GeoJSON Upload

Rivers, lakes, and other water bodies. Used to flag detections near water sources — illegal mining near water causes mercury and sediment contamination.

Drop GeoJSON file here or click to browse

Accepted: .geojson, .json

About this data

Water body boundaries are stored as monitoring regions. On the live map they can be toggled as a layer to visualise proximity of illegal mining to rivers and lakes.

Uploading replaces all previously imported water body regions. Feature property name is used as the region name.

Source suggestion: Ghana Water Resources Commission, OpenStreetMap rivers export, or HydroSHEDS.

Protected Forest Boundaries — GeoJSON Upload

Ghana Forestry Commission forest reserves, national parks, and IUCN protected areas. Detections inside protected forests are treated as higher-priority violations.

Drop GeoJSON file here or click to browse

Accepted: .geojson, .json

About this data

Protected forest boundaries define high-sensitivity monitoring zones. Any detection falling inside a forest reserve or national park can be automatically flagged as a critical alert.

Uploading replaces all previously imported protected forest regions. Feature property name is used as the region name.

Source suggestion: Ghana Forestry Commission, WDPA (Protected Planet), or Global Forest Watch.
